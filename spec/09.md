# 09. Nameserver and Registrar Workflows

## TLDR

Nameserver reassignment is a registrar workflow that updates parent-zone delegation and must be verified separately from hosted DNS records.

## Delegation Model

Authoritative delegation is controlled by the registrar or registry-facing domain API. Donazopy must not treat zone-file NS record edits as sufficient for changing domain delegation.

## Workflow

A nameserver reassignment plan should read current registrar nameservers, validate target nameserver syntax, check glue requirements, update registrar delegation through the provider adapter, and verify the parent-zone result through RDAP, WHOIS, or DNS queries after propagation.

## Provider Differences

Cloudflare registrar, Route 53 Domains, IONOS domains, Joker DMAPI, GoDaddy, Namecheap, DNSimple, Gandi, Porkbun, and Dynadot each have distinct delegation semantics. DNS-only providers such as Vercel or DigitalOcean cannot reassign parent-zone nameservers unless they also control the domain registration.

## Acceptance Criteria

- Delegation commands require `delegation_write` capability.
- DNS-only providers refuse registrar operations.
- Verification reports parent-zone delegation status, not just child-zone NS records.
